# Economics

There are a range of costs, problems and challenges for anyone who runs a data centre, in scales from the medium enterprise, through large multi-national companies, and on to multi-tenant data centres. When we buy the hardware to operate services, there is an initial capital expenditure, referred to as CAPEX. There is also the on-going expense, the operational expense of running the services, known as OPEX. In most cases, after one to two years of operation, the OPEX of running services exceeds the CAPEX of the equipment purchased in the first place. 

By designing with virtualization in mind, we can buy a great deal less hardware and have a much smaller footprint when we look at the CAPEX of setting up a data centre. 

There are several components to OPEX, typically; 

- Space and facilities
- Power
- Cooling
- Service and maintenance
- Disposal of obsolete equipment
- Backup and DR
- Equipment replacement costs
- Insurance
- Security 

In a well-planned virtualized environment, these factors show savings with cloud techniques. 

On the first major virtualization project I was involved in, the enterprise had +80 servers many of which were obsolete and needed replacement. The proposal was to replace them with far fewer hosts, paying the CAPEX costs over 18 months with saving in OPEX, originally costed as power only. The original proposal was conservative, the long-term saving was greater than originally proposed, mostly due to additional savings on cooling. This was a server consolidation project, eliminating the over-provisioning of hardware.
