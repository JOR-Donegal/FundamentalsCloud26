# Types of Cloud

There are fours main ways we classify cloud computing. 

## Private Cloud
One is called _Private Cloud_. This is where a company runs all its own equipment and controls everything themselves. Because everything is virtual, they can be very flexible and responsive, and they can do everything very economically. Surveys show that around 90% of all spend and effort is still in enterprise and private clouds, even though it does not get as much press. 

## Public Cloud
The other option is _Public Cloud_. When you use a G Drive and G Mail, or One Drive and Hotmail, you are using public cloud services. Some of these services are free, but for business we pay for them. Public cloud allows us to build up very complex services and on-line products, without having any hardware or premises at all. Everything is somewhere “_in the cloud_”. Companies like Amazon, Google and Microsoft have enormous multi-tenant data centres, built specially for other people to use. With nothing more than a credit card and Wi-Fi access, I can build an on-line service in minutes. We say the public cloud is _elastic_, I can make it grow as big and as powerful as I need when demand increases. Start-ups will use public cloud, as will developers who are creating new services and do not want the cost of infrastructure.

Imagine the sales model of a toy shop. In Europe, no sales in January, small amounts of sales all the year round and 90% of all sales in 3 weeks in December. Public cloud is ideal for dealing with capacity requirements; run for 11 months a year in economical private cloud and for 1 month, take the extra capacity from (the more expensive) public cloud. There are other options.

## Community Cloud
With a _Community Cloud_, the cloud infrastructure is provisioned for exclusive use by a specific community of consumers from organizations that have shared concerns (e.g., mission, security requirements, policy, and compliance considerations). It may be owned, managed, and operated by one or more of the organizations in the community, a third party, or some combination of them, and it may exist on or off premises. In most countries, we have a _national research and education network_ (NREN) which offers community cloud services. Most videos we publish for teaching come from https://media.hetnet.ie, this is a perfect example.

## Hybrid Cloud
With a __Hybrid Cloud__, the cloud infrastructure is a composition of two or more distinct cloud infrastructures (private, community, or public) that remain unique entities. They are bound together by standardized or proprietary technology that enables data and application portability.
